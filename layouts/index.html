Data Trust Engineering (DTE)

Engineering Trust for AI-Ready Data. Data Trust Engineering (DTE) is a movement to replace traditional data governance’s 70-80% failure rate [Gartner, 2025] with engineering-driven trust, certification, and AI-readiness. Born from the Data Trust Engineering Manifesto, DTE certifies data systems by use case, risk, and value through community collaboration, sidestepping bureaucratic governance. By decoupling from compliance and leveraging modern engineering best practices, DTE offers a modern replacement for data governance. Our first artifact, the DTE Trust Dashboard, visualizes AI governance metrics—more are coming! Join us at datatrustmanifesto.org and fuel the #DTERevolution.

Evolution of Data Governance: From Enron to Buzzword

  • 2001: Enron Scandal — Massive accounting fraud collapses investor trust.
  • 2002: Sarbanes–Oxley Act (SOX) — Introduces controls, accountability, and auditor oversight (PCAOB).
  • 2003-2008: Consulting Expansion — PwC, Deloitte, Accenture generalize governance beyond finance to master data, metadata, and quality.
  • 2009: DAMA-DMBOK & Gartner Models — Formalized frameworks and “Enterprise Data Governance” maturity models emerge. (DAMA, Gartner, IBM, CMMI, EDM)
  • 2010s-2020s: Cloud & Big Data Era — Governance stretches to privacy, AI ethics, and security; vendors rebrand catalogs and lineage as “governance.”
  • Today: Nebulous Buzzword — Governance means everything from compliance to AI fairness, becoming dangerously vague.

Where do we go from here? DTE moves beyond governing data to certifying it based on use case, risk, and value, delivering trusted, AI-ready systems.
