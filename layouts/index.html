Data Trust Engineering (DTE)

Engineering Trust for AI-Ready Data. Data Trust Engineering (DTE) is a movement to replace traditional data governance’s 70-80% failure rate [Gartner, 2025] with engineering-driven trust, certification, and AI-readiness. Born from the Data Trust Engineering Manifesto, DTE certifies data systems by use case, risk, and value through community collaboration, sidestepping bureaucratic governance. By decoupling from compliance and leveraging modern engineering best practices, DTE offers a modern replacement for data governance. Our first artifact, the DTE Trust Dashboard, visualizes AI governance metrics—more are coming! Join us at datatrustmanifesto.org and fuel the #DTERevolution.

Evolution of Data Governance: From Enron to a Catch-All

  • 2001: Enron Scandal — Corporate fraud shakes investor trust worldwide.
  • 2002: Sarbanes–Oxley Act (SOX) — Establishes stronger controls, accountability, and independent oversight (PCAOB).
  • 2003–2008: Consulting Expansion — Major firms (PwC, Deloitte, Accenture) extend governance concepts from finance into master data, metadata, and quality management.
  • 2009: DAMA-DMBOK & Gartner Models — Formal frameworks and enterprise maturity models take hold, setting a common vocabulary for governance (DAMA, Gartner, IBM, CMMI, EDM).
  • 2010s–2020s: Cloud & Big Data Era — Governance expands to cover privacy, security, and AI ethics, while vendors rebrand catalogs, lineage, and quality tools as “data governance.”
  • Today: A Catch-All Term — Governance is invoked to mean everything from compliance to AI fairness. This breadth has made the term broad but often difficult to apply in practice.

Where do we go from here? Data Trust Engineering (DTE) reframes the challenge: moving from governing data in the abstract to certifying it by use case, risk, and value — delivering trusted, AI-ready systems.
